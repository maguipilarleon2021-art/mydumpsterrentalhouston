Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can we dispose of grease trap waste or interceptor sludge in your commercial dumpsters?

No, grease trap waste cannot be placed in standard solid waste containers. Liquid fats, oils, and grease must be pumped and transported by a specialized vacuum truck to comply with environmental laws in East Grand Rapids, MI. Our dumpsters are strictly for solid food waste, packaging, and standard garbage.

2. What is the "1/4 rule" and does it apply to our solid waste dumpsters?

The 1/4 rule typically applies to grease interceptors, mandating a pump-out when the tank is 25 percent full of solids and grease. For our solid waste dumpsters in East Grand Rapids, MI, we recommend scheduling a swap or pickup when the debris is level with the top rim to prevent spillage, pest infestations, and rejected pickups.

3. Do you provide waste disposal manifests for restaurant health inspections?

Yes, tracking your commercial waste is critical for passing municipal health and safety audits. We provide detailed digital invoices and disposal manifests for every pickup in East Grand Rapids, MI, ensuring you have the proper documentation to prove your business remains fully compliant with local sanitation codes.

4. How quickly can you empty our bin if we have an unexpected trash backup?

We understand that a busy weekend rush can generate significantly more refuse than anticipated. If you experience an overflow, contact our local dispatch in East Grand Rapids, MI immediately. We offer emergency, same-day dispatch routing whenever possible to eliminate odors and keep your parking lot completely clean.

5. Are there specific items from our commercial kitchen that are prohibited?

Absolutely. Aside from liquid cooking oils and grease trap sludge, you must also exclude liquid cleaning chemicals, industrial solvents, and electronic waste. Sticking to approved solid waste ensures your garbage dumpster service in East Grand Rapids, MI operates safely and avoids hazardous material fines.
