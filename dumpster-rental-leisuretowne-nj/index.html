Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can we dispose of grease trap waste or interceptor sludge in your commercial dumpsters?

No, grease trap waste cannot be placed in standard solid waste containers. Liquid fats, oils, and grease must be pumped and transported by a specialized vacuum truck to comply with environmental laws in Leisuretowne, NJ. Our dumpsters are strictly for solid food waste, packaging, and standard garbage.

2. What is the "1/4 rule" and does it apply to our solid waste dumpsters?

The 1/4 rule typically applies to grease interceptors, mandating a pump-out when the tank is 25 percent full of solids and grease. For our solid waste dumpsters in Leisuretowne, NJ, we recommend scheduling a swap or pickup when the debris is level with the top rim to prevent spillage, pest infestations, and rejected pickups.

Yes, tracking your commercial waste is critical for passing municipal health and safety audits. We provide detailed digital invoices and disposal manifests for every pickup in Leisuretowne, NJ, ensuring you have the proper documentation to prove your business remains fully compliant with local sanitation codes.

We understand that a busy weekend rush can generate significantly more refuse than anticipated. If you experience an overflow, contact our local dispatch in Leisuretowne, NJ immediately. We offer emergency, same-day dispatch routing whenever possible to eliminate odors and keep your parking lot completely clean.

5. Are there specific items from our commercial kitchen that are prohibited?

Absolutely. Aside from liquid cooking oils and grease trap sludge, you must also exclude liquid cleaning chemicals, industrial solvents, and electronic waste.
